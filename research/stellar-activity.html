Stellar Activity, Rotation & Magnetism

Magnetic activity and rotation as both astrophysical signals and sources of noise in precision stellar and planetary measurements.

Back to research cards Publications on ADS

Activity as physics

Magnetic activity is not just a nuisance: starspots, plages, flares, and magnetically structured atmospheres reveal how stellar dynamos operate. In active stars and close binaries, the geometry and evolution of magnetic regions can be especially informative.

Activity as a measurement challenge

Rotation and activity also complicate precision work. They can imitate or mask oscillations, change apparent stellar parameters, and affect the interpretation of exoplanet host stars. That makes careful time-series analysis central to both stellar astrophysics and exoplanet characterization.

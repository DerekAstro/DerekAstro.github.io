About

Stars, signals, and students

I am an astronomer and professor in the Department of Astronomy and Astrophysics at the University of Chicago. My work uses precise time-series measurements — especially photometry and polarimetry — to understand how stars behave and what that behavior reveals about their interiors, atmospheres, and environments.

Public profiles and publication records connect my work with asteroseismology, stellar magnetic activity, exoplanets, WIRE, Kepler, TESS, and optical/ultraviolet instrumentation. This site is meant as a flexible starting point and can be expanded with a CV, course pages, software, student projects, observing notes, and selected papers.
